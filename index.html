Hi, I'm Brittany. I'm a creative problem-solver & a tireless dream chaser. Also, I love to code. Nice to meet you!

All about that perspective.

My education and jump into development isn't the most traditional. I have a B.A. in social sciences and a scattered BC (before computers) resume of accounting, marketing, customer service, and record keeping roles. I taught myself how to code in my nights and weekends after college when I realized after one search that web dev combines all the things I love in life. After a year or so of freelancing on top of my day job, I won a full ride scholarship to attend an immersive full stack bootcamp in Seattle, WA. So, I quit the job I disliked and went headfirst towards a career as a full time web developer.


I love learning how all the parts of websites and apps work together. Though, to be honest the front-end feels like an extension of my brain's thought process, it feels like home, so I lean that way. My educational background gives me a holistic approach to projects while my fast-paced education and "do or die" mentality gives me the energy and determination I need to take on any project laid in front of me.

Some faves

CSS3 & SASS

I believe in a responsive foundation - this means a strong outline (HTML) and stylesheets(CSS/SASS). I take pride in knowing my work can be viewed and enjoyed on a variety of screens. It's pretty cool to me what all can be accomplished with good CSS3. I love playing with transitions, animations, and typography in my downtime, and sometimes just for kicks I'll do things like make a css BB8 from Star Wars.

Javascript

Javascript (and jQuery) is an integral part of the modern web, and I love using it for anything ranging from animation to data manipulation. I have experience writing my own scripts as well as using popular plug-ins, like fullpageJS, parallax, and fitText. Most recently, I've been developing admin tools in a javascript library called Kendo UI. I think it's important to know how to do things yourself, but I truly value the aid of a good framework so that you can have more time to do new things and improve upon that which already exsists.

Frameworks

I am a fan of Angular 2, but I see the usefulness of React and look forward to using it on future projects. How data binds in both of these tools is such an interesting philosophical discussion of use. I have built several client-side applications with AngularJS (I'm working on converting one of them to Angular 2 currently), including my final project for the General Assembly Immersive bootcamp I took in the fall of 2015. With javascript frameworks, there's always something new to learn which is very exciting.

Inclusivity

I don't think it's enough to simply make things responsive for the current day and age. I believe in making things dynamic for future screens by using relative units, as well as making sure things are backwards compatible within the given userbase. I enjoy learning about developing with aria, screen types, and compatibility and am always trying to incorporate more of this into my work. I believe in putting my best self into the work that I do.

RSVPtree

A Full-Stack App

RSVPtree is a Node app built with ExpressJS. It streamlines the RSVP process for big parties like weddings and family reunions. It uses Sequelize to track the database and form information of various parties and keep them separate while focusing on what information is needed from guests to efficiently plan a party. It's refreshingly simple to use as a guest. But the best part about it is how it forms family trees to help learn family connections better. This comes in handy if the user doesn't know their better half's extended family super well!

Technologies used

  • Express
  • Node.js
  • Sequelize
  • Heroku
  • Postgres
  • oAuth
  • HTML5
  • CSS3
  • Javascript
  • MaterializeCSS
