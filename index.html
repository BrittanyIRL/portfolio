Hi, I'm Brittany. I'm a Seattle-based front end developer who delights in creative problem solvings, strong code, and out of the box thinking. I am #blessed to be doing what I love.

I'm a full stack developer. But, that's pretty broad, so here's a few things I can't get enough of.

HTML5 & CSS3

I believe in a responsive foundation - which means strong HTML5 and CSS3. I take pride in knowing my work can be viewed on a variety of screens and sizes. It is truly amazing what all can be accomplished with good CSS3, I love playing with transitions, animations, and typography in my downtime.

jQuery

jQuery is an amazing tool which I love working with. It's powerful and lightweight, allowing us to accomplish a variety of means with it - from animation to data manipulation. I have experience writing my own scripts as well as using popular plugins, like fullpageJS, parallax, and more.

AngularJS

I love how abstract AngularJS is. The beauty of a 1 page application in the MEAN stack inspires me so much.

NodeJS

In a lot of ways NodeJS is the opposite of AngularJS, but it's equally facinating to work with. Given it's more traditional full stack set up, it gives you a lot more oomph for building database heavy structures. I particularly enjoy working with Express, EJS and Sequelize with my Node apps.

The possibilities of the web are unlimited, these are a few of my favorite contributions I've made lately.

RSVPtree

A Full-Stack Express App

RSVPtree is an expressJS app built with the intention of streamlining the RSVP process for big parties like weddings and family reunions. It uses sequelize to track the database and form information of various parties and keep them seperate while focusing on what information is needed from guests to efficiently plan a party. The cool part about it, besides how easy it is to use as a guest, is the family tree(s) it will help you make to learn family connections better - which comes in hand for weddings especially if the bride or groom doesn't know their better half's extended family super well.

Technologies used

  • Express
  • Node.JS
  • Sequelize
  • Heroku
  • PostgreSQL
  • oAuth
  • HTML5/CSS3/Javascript
  • MaterializeCSS

Explor

Travel App Built with Ruby on Rails

Explor is a travel app built in 1 week with 2 other web developer students. It is an API heavy application that fuels the wanderer inside us all. Explor was built with the Ruby on Rails Stack and with the intention to be easily added to for more robust functionality.
